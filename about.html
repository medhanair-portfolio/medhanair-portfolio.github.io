About Me

I am a media & journalism and anthropology double major at UNC-Chapel Hill. I strive to spotlight untold or undertold stories. If these stories can impact at least one person, its purpose is complete. The people I meet inspire me the most.

My family is from Kerala, India and my parents immigrated to Brooklyn, New York in 2000 to start a new life. Since then they have been teaching me the intricacies of the world. Gradually, from my first day of school, my first job, and my first year of college at Macaulay Honors College -- I have been exposed to people from all walks of life. That is what inspired me to pursue anthropology and telling stories has always been a passion.

Now, as a journalism student I wish to write on a variety of topics and grow my skills to become a better writer. Multimedia projects are also something I wish to pursue because a good story deserves to be told in a variety of ways. As a writer on the lifestyle desk at the Daily Tar Heel, I wrote stories about the community from mental health clinics to drag shows. Now I write stories on the city and state desk.
